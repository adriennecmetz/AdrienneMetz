Bio

About

Adrienne Metz

Connect

A life long seeker of new challenges, I embarked on my coding journey in the summer of 2022 with self study in javascript. In the early 2000’s as a producer with Fox News in NYC, I volunteered to take a course in HTML to oversee the weekly newsletter. At this time I had also studied and knew AVID editing software and spent my days in edit bays producing on air promos and news segments. By 2007, I had moved across the country to Los Angeles where I worked with large film companies to produce Hollywood blockbuster comedies with the Mosaic Media Group and supervised and produced non scripted television series for many television networks. In 2011, I left the 24/7 pace of film and television production and took a break to raise my two children with my husband near the beach in San Clemente, California. During this time I founded and helmed the start up Chemo Beanies. Having identified a unique market, I designed and developed a a manufacturing line based in Los Angeles while setting up distribution channels to resource centers for women with cancer including Cancer Centers of America, Mayo Clinic and Scripps Hospital Network.  In 2013, I was recognized by Chase Bank as an influential women entrepreneur and was awarded a 2013 Mission on Main Street Grant. I have remained active in my community, creating graphic and web services for our local school foundation and running their online giving/auction software as a volunteer. With a passion for computers, design and learning the pursuit of my current journey feels like a natural progression for me. In the winter of 2023 I will have completed the MIT x PRO Professional Certificate of Coding: Full Stack Development with MERN.
